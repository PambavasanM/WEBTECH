Types Of Photography Allowed

Portrait photography

Portrait photography captures the emotion, expression, and personality of individuals or groups. Often focused on faces, it uses lighting and composition to highlight features. It's widely used in professional headshots, family photos, and creative personal projects.


Landscape photography

Landscape photography showcases the natural beauty of outdoor environments like mountains, rivers, forests, and skies. It often uses wide-angle lenses and natural lighting to create visually stunning scenes that reflect the serenity, power, or grandeur of nature.


Wildlife photography

Wildlife photography focuses on animals in their natural habitats. It requires patience, timing, and specialized lenses to capture rare behaviors and majestic creatures. Often used in documentaries and conservation efforts, it brings viewers closer to the animal kingdom.


Astrophotography

Astrophotography captures celestial objects like stars, planets, and galaxies. Using long exposures and telescopic lenses, photographers reveal the beauty of the night sky. It blends art and science, offering breathtaking views of the universe beyond human eyesight.


Food photography

Food photography presents meals in a visually appetizing way. With the right lighting, angles, and styling, it makes dishes look delicious and attractive. Commonly used in menus, blogs, and advertising, it stimulates hunger and promotes culinary experiences.
